About Portland Reuse Hub

üå± What Is Portland Reuse Hub?

We help Portland residents find the right place for any item they want to donate, repair, recycle, or properly dispose of—reducing waste and supporting our circular economy.

Our directory currently lists 30 verified locations across the Portland metro area, from nonprofits like Free Geek and the ReBuilding Center to city facilities like Metro South Transfer Station.

üìñ How to Use This Site

  1. Search for your item in the search bar—try "laptop", "couch", or "paint".
  2. Browse by category using the Popular Items chips or Category filters below the search bar.
  3. Filter by type—Donation, Repair, Recycling, or Disposal—to narrow your results.
  4. Explore the map to find locations near you. Enable location access to see distances.
  5. Click any location to see hours, accepted items, and get driving directions.

üìÇ Understanding the Categories

Donation

Give away usable items in good condition so they get a second life.

Examples: Furniture, clothing, electronics, books, toys, building materials

üìç Goodwill, Habitat ReStore, SCRAP PDX, Community Cycling Center

Repair

Fix broken items instead of throwing them away. Many events are free!

Examples: Electronics, bikes, appliances, clothing, furniture

üìç Repair PDX, Portland Repair Collective

Recycling

Process materials to be made into new products, keeping them out of landfills.

Examples: Paper, cardboard, electronics, metals, batteries, styrofoam

üìç Far West Fibers, Next Step Recycling, The Bin

Disposal

Proper disposal for hazardous or special waste that can't be donated or recycled.

Examples: Paint, chemicals, batteries, motor oil, medications

üìç Metro South Transfer Station, Portland Composts!
